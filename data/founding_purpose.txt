Gujarat Vidyapith was established as an alternative to British colonial education system. During the Non-cooperation Movement of 1920, Gandhiji called for boycott of British government schools and colleges. Gujarat Vidyapith was one of five national Vidyapiths established to provide education based on Indian culture, values of self-reliance, simplicity, and truth and non-violence. The institution aimed to prepare workers with character, ability, culture and dedication for national regeneration according to Gandhian ideals.
