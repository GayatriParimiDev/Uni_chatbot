Gujarat Vidyapith was founded by Mahatma Gandhi on October 18, 1920 in Ahmedabad, Gujarat. It was established as a Rashtriya Vidyapith (National Institute) during the Non-cooperation Movement. Mahatma Gandhi served as its life-long Chancellor (Kulpati) from 1920 until his death in 1948. Professor A.T. Gidwani was its founder Vice-Chancellor. In July 1963, it was recognized as a "deemed to be University" by the University Grants Commission (UGC) under Section 3 of the UGC Act, 1956.
